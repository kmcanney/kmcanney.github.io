Busses - overview

You can use busses to group and mix tracks. The most common uses of busses are for routing tracks and effects outputs to specific hardware outputs or simply to use a bus as a master control for a set of tracks.

For example, if you wanted to control the master level of all your drum tracks to a relative level, you could create a bus and assign all drum tracks to that bus. After your drums are mixed, you can adjust the overall volume of the drum tracks by adjusting the bus volume.

You can also use busses to create channel mappings for rendering multichannel audio files. For more information, see "Rendering Multichannel Audio Files."

To see the audio signal flow, see "Signal flow diagram."

Creating a Picture-in-Picture Effect

You can use Track Motion to create and animate picture-in-picture effects. For more information, see "Editing track motion."

Let's create a simple two-video picture-in-picture project.

  1. Create a new project file.

  2. Add media to the project. Your media can come from any number of sources:

    • You can add media files from your computer.

    • You can record audio for dialog, voiceovers, music tracks, or sound effects.

    • You can capture video from a video camera.

    • You can import video from a DVD camcorder.

    • You can download media from the Web.

  3. Add your media files to the timeline. The video that you want to display in a window should be on the top track, and the background video should be placed on a lower track.

  4. Click the Track Motion button on the top track. The Track Motion dialog is displayed.

  5. Adjust the selection area to change the viewable area of the track and its position. You can use the Position controls on the left side of the window, or you can drag the selection box in the workspace.

    For this example, let's drag the bottom-left corner of the box to reduce the size of the video window and then drag the center of the box to position it in the upper-right corner of the Video Preview window.

  6. If you want to change the position of the video window over time (to shrink and fly to the upper-right corner of the video frame, for example), you can use the keyframe controller at the bottom of the Track Motion window to establish distinct track motion settings throughout the duration of Track 1.

  7. If you want to set off your inset picture, select the 2D Shadow or 2D Glow check box at the bottom of the Track Motion window and use the 2D Shadow or 2D Glow controls on the left side of the Track Motion dialog to customize your shadow or glow effect.
